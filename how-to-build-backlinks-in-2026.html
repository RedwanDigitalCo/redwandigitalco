What Are Backlinks and Why Do They Matter?

A backlink (also called an inbound link or incoming link) is simply a link from one website to another. When Website A links to your website, you've earned a backlink from Website A.

Think of it like a recommendation letter. When a trusted person vouches for you, people take you more seriously. That's exactly how Google treats backlinks — each one is a vote of confidence from another website saying "this content is worth reading."

In 2026, backlinks remain one of Google's top three ranking signals alongside content quality and search intent. Without them, even the best-written blog posts can struggle to rank — especially in competitive niches.

💡 Good news for beginners: You don't need hundreds of backlinks to start seeing results. For low-competition keywords, even 5–15 quality links can push you onto the first page of Google.

Types of Backlinks: Dofollow vs Nofollow

Not every backlink works the same way. Before you start building links, it helps to understand the two main types.

Dofollow Backlinks

These are the most valuable. A dofollow link passes SEO authority (often called "link juice") from the linking website to yours. When a high-authority site gives you a dofollow link, your domain authority grows and your pages are more likely to rank higher.

Nofollow Backlinks

A nofollow link tells Google not to pass authority through the link. They don't directly boost rankings, but they still drive real visitors to your website and make your link profile look natural — which Google actually prefers. A healthy mix of both is ideal.

Other Link Types in 2026

Sponsored links are tagged on paid placements. UGC links (User Generated Content) appear in forums and comment sections. Google handles all these differently, but for a new site, your focus should be on earning genuine dofollow links from relevant, trusted sources.

✅ Key insight: Domain relevance matters as much as domain authority. A dofollow link from a smaller but related website beats a nofollow link from a massive unrelated platform.

Low KD Keywords to Target for Link Building Content

One of the smartest moves for a new website is to target low keyword difficulty (KD) topics when writing content designed to attract backlinks. Instead of competing for "how to build backlinks" (high KD, dominated by Ahrefs, Semrush, and Backlinko), go for longer, more specific variations that bigger sites ignore.

By targeting these specific phrases, you attract readers who are more likely to share your content, reference it in their own blogs, and naturally link back to you — which is the most sustainable form of link building.

10 Easy Backlink Building Strategies for Beginners

Here are ten proven methods that work especially well for new sites and small businesses in 2026. Start with the easiest ones first and build from there.

01
Easy

Google Business Profile & Directory Listings

Claim your free Google Business Profile. Submit to Bing Places, Yelp, and niche-specific directories. These give you immediate, authoritative backlinks with zero cost and minimal effort.

02
Easy

Social Media Profiles

Create complete profiles on Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Pinterest, and YouTube. Most of these are nofollow, but they establish brand credibility and drive traffic that leads to organic links.

03
Easy

Guest Posting on Small Blogs

Don't target giant sites yet. Find small-to-medium blogs in your niche and offer to write a helpful article. Most accept guest posts and will give you a dofollow author link in return.

04
Easy

Answer Questions on Quora & Reddit

Find questions related to your niche and provide genuinely helpful answers. Link to your relevant blog posts where appropriate. These drive real traffic and nofollow links that diversify your profile.

05
Medium

Create Shareable Infographics

A well-designed infographic that simplifies a complex topic gets shared and embedded by other bloggers — who will almost always link back to the original source (your website).

06
Easy

Resource Page Link Building

Many websites maintain "useful resources" or "recommended tools" pages. Search Google for your niche + "useful resources" and email site owners asking to be included. Conversion rate is surprisingly high.

07
Medium

Broken Link Building

Use free tools like Check My Links (Chrome extension) to find broken links on relevant websites. Reach out to the owner, let them know, and suggest your content as a replacement. Win-win.

08
Easy

Local Citations & Niche Directories

Submit your business to local chambers of commerce websites, industry association directories, and local news websites. These are trusted, geographically relevant backlinks that help local SEO enormously.

09
Medium

Media Outreach (HARO Alternative)

HARO was rebranded and disrupted — use Connectively, Qwoted, or SourceBottle instead. Respond to journalist requests in your niche and earn high-authority links from news sites when quoted.

10
Medium

Create Original Data or Research

Publish a small survey or original study in your niche. Even a simple "we asked 50 small business owners..." post attracts links from bloggers who cite your data as a reference.

💡 Pro Tip for New Websites: Start with strategies 1, 2, 4, 6, and 8. These require zero budget, can be done in your first week, and instantly build a healthy, diverse link profile that signals legitimacy to Google.

Using LSI Keywords to Attract Natural Backlinks

LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords are words and phrases that are semantically related to your main topic. When you naturally include LSI keywords throughout your content, Google better understands what your page is about — and ranks it for a broader range of related searches.

More importantly, content that covers a topic comprehensively using LSI keywords tends to earn more natural backlinks because it becomes a one-stop reference. Other writers link to complete, authoritative resources rather than thin articles that only answer one question.

🔗 LSI Keywords for "How to Build Backlinks"
link building strategies inbound links domain authority referring domains anchor text link juice organic link building white hat SEO search engine ranking website authority guest blogging editorial links nofollow dofollow link profile natural link acquisition off-page SEO external links citation building link outreach content marketing for links

How to Use LSI Keywords in Your Content

  • 1
    Don't force them. LSI keywords should flow naturally in your writing. Read your content out loud — if a phrase sounds unnatural, rewrite it.
  • 2
    Use them in subheadings (H2/H3). Including LSI terms in headings helps Google connect your content to related topics without keyword stuffing.
  • 3
    Cover the topic fully. LSI works best when your article genuinely answers all related questions. Think of your blog post as the most helpful resource on the topic.
  • 4
    Find LSI keywords using Google. Type your main keyword in Google and scroll to the "People also ask" and "Related searches" sections. Those are your LSI keywords, given to you for free.

Backlink Mistakes to Avoid in 2026

Building the wrong kind of backlinks can do more damage than having none at all. Here are the most common mistakes beginners make — and how to avoid them.

⚠️ Never buy backlinks. Paid link schemes are a direct violation of Google's Spam Policies. If caught, your site can receive a manual penalty that wipes it from search results entirely. It's not worth the risk — especially for a new website with no authority to protect itself.

Other Mistakes to Avoid

Getting links from irrelevant sites. A backlink from a cooking blog won't help your digital marketing website. Relevance is a core part of how Google evaluates link quality in 2026.

Using exact-match anchor text repeatedly. If every single backlink to your site uses the exact phrase "how to build backlinks," it looks manipulated. Vary your anchor text naturally — use brand names, URLs, and partial match phrases too.

Ignoring toxic backlinks. Low-quality spammy sites sometimes link to you without permission. Check your backlink profile monthly using Google Search Console or a free tool like Ubersuggest. Disavow any suspicious links.

Building links too fast. Gaining 200 backlinks in a week when you're a new site looks unnatural to Google. A slow, consistent growth rate — even 5–10 quality links per month — is healthier and more sustainable long-term.

How Long Does It Take to See Results?

This is the question everyone asks — and the honest answer is: it depends. But here's a realistic timeline for a new website focusing on low-competition keywords with consistent link building.

  • 1
    Weeks 1–2: Set up all free directory listings and social profiles. Your first 10–15 backlinks are built. Google starts discovering your site.
  • 2
    Weeks 3–6: Begin guest posting and forum participation. You'll see your first impressions appear in Google Search Console. Low-competition long-tail keywords may start showing on page 2–3.
  • 3
    Months 2–3: With 20–40 quality backlinks and consistent content, you should see page 1 rankings for your lowest-difficulty keywords. Organic traffic begins to grow noticeably.
  • 4
    Months 4–6: Medium-difficulty keywords become achievable. Your domain authority climbs. Each new backlink now carries more weight because your site has established trust.
✅ Remember: Consistency beats intensity. 10 quality backlinks per month for 6 months is far more effective than 200 low-quality links in one week. Google rewards patience and genuine authority building.
